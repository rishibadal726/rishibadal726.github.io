3.1: Why does the least significant bit position use a half-adder rather than a full-adder?

The least significant bit position uses a half-adder rather than a full-adder is becuase a full-adder can only be used with two single-bit binary integers plus a carry.

We can infer that the non-scientific calculator used a ripple carry-adder, allowing it to produce the high output. This state is commonly called cascading adders.
